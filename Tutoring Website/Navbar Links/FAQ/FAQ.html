    • FAQ

    Here are our most commonly asked questions!

    Q: Where do Tutors and Students Meet?

    A: CLTutoring offers a online tutoring environment for students, however, if you prefer in-person, our tutors are usually available to meet up in many places, like a library, starbucks, the student/tutor's home, or other quiet, study locations. Tutors are very flexible, and are always offering options to provide maximum convinience for the student.

    Q: Can I get Tutored for a Subject thats not Listed?

    A: Yes! If you have a subject in mind that is not listed in the Subjects We Teach Page, please let us know! Our tutors are willing to help you wherever we can, and we want YOU to succeed.

    Q: How much Money does it Cost for Tutoring?

    A: NONE! CLTutoring tutors students for free at the moment, so try us out! No matter what you need help with, we want to give back to our community, even if you just need advice on whether to take a class or not.

    Q: Is Becoming a Tutor a lot of Work?

    A: Being a tutor at CLTutoring requires committment and dedication. CLTutoring is very flexible, so if you have concerns, such as being unavailable to tutor from 9am-6pm, we try our best to work with you. Please don't be afraid to apply, and talk to us if you have any questions.

    Q: How is CLTutoring different and what are the Benefits from Being a Student here?

    A: CLTutoring is different from other tutoring services in Charlotte. Like other tutoring organizations, CLTutoring offers to help with homework and anything else that is school related. However, we also guide students with advice, inform students about classes, and use our experience to your benefit.

    Q: What are the Benefits from Becoming a Tutor?

    A: Being a tutor at CLTutoring is something that you can put on your college resume, and you can include that you like to give back to your community and provided tutoring services to students in various subjects. However, being apart of CLTutoring requires work to be put in.

    Q: How Proficient do you need to be at a Subject to Tutor it?

    A: You need to have enough knowledge in a subject to be able to help someone else with their homework. During the interview to apply to tutor subjects, you will be asked how well you know your subject.

    Q: Who made this Website?

    A: Ayush Bhujle coded this website, using programming languages of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. All 3 of these subjects are tutored here so you can learn to how computer science and programming works.
